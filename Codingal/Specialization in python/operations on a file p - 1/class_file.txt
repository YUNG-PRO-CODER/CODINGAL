Playboi Carti (Jordan Terrell Carter) and Ken Carson (Kenyatta Lee Frazier Jr.) 
form a synergistic duo within Atlanta’s modern hip-hop scene, 
combining avant-garde sound, fashion influence, and the strategic branding of the Opium label. 
Their collaboration exemplifies a cross-generational and stylistic blend: 
Carti embodies the enigmatic, experimental trap innovator, while Ken Carson channels the next wave of rage-infused, 
high-energy hip-hop, bridging legacy with contemporary flair.

Playboi Carti: Known for his “baby voice,” erratic flows, and experimental trap techniques. 
Signature projects include Die Lit (2018), Whole Lotta Red (2020), and MUSIC (2025). 
Carti is recognized for running the Opium label, curating artists’ careers while occasionally executing strategic features.

Ken Carson: Emerges from Carti's guidance but carves his own identity with a darker, 
emo-trap sound, aggressive ad-libs, and thematic focus on anxiety, heartbreak, and chaotic energy. 
Albums include Project X (2021), X (2022), A Great Chaos (2023), and More Chaos (2025), 
gaining both critical and commercial acclaim.

Collaborations such as “Off The Meter” feature Carti alongside Ken Carson and Destroy Lonely, 
fusing Carti’s avant-garde vocal experimentation with Ken’s energetic, melodic trap layers.

Their live performances and festival appearances, including ComplexCon 2026, 
are designed as immersive Opium experiences, where Carti takes artistic director responsibilities, 
and Ken exemplifies the label’s youthful energy.
